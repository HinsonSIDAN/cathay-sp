The partner SP matrix has a **floor**: even a 200-mile hop pays 15 SP in a business-tier
class and 15 SP in first-tier ([[earning/partner-model]]). Domestic markets where premium
cabins are cheap turn that floor into the best SP-per-dollar in the whole system.

## JAL domestic Class J (国内線クラスJ)

JAL's domestic "Class J" is a modest recliner upsell over economy — but it books into
**E, which Cathay credits as business tier B** (verified: [[airlines/japan-airlines]]).

| Sector example | Distance / zone | SP per sector | Indicative one-way cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| HND⇄ITM (Tokyo–Osaka) | ~280mi, Ultra-short | **15** | JPY 12,000–18,000 (~HK$600–900) |
| HND⇄CTS (Tokyo–Sapporo) | 511mi, Ultra-short ([[routes/hnd-cts]]) | **15** | similar |
| HND⇄OKA (Tokyo–Okinawa) | 966mi, **Short** | **25** | slightly more |

- A Tokyo⇄Osaka round trip = 30 SP for ~HK$1,200–1,800 — roughly **HK$40–60 per SP**,
  several times cheaper than any long-haul business run.
- HND⇄OKA crosses the 750-mile boundary into Short: **25 SP per sector** — the single
  best SP-per-dollar sector known (≈HK$36–40/SP).
- JAL domestic **First (F)** earns tier A (same 15/25 SP at these zones) for a small
  premium — Class J is usually the better buy since SP is equal.
- Book **JL flight numbers** (JL-marketed); a CX-codeshare booking would earn from the
  CX feeder table instead.
- Pairs naturally with a Japan trip you were taking anyway — the Aug-2025 Short-T2 boost
  already makes HKG⇄Japan CX business the strongest regional earner (45 SP/sector).

## AA domestic First

US domestic First on American is often US$150–250 one-way if bought early, and books
into **A/F — first tier A** (verified: [[airlines/american-airlines]]):

| Sector example | Zone | SP round trip |
|---|---|---|
| SFO⇄LAX (cf. [DFW–LAX entry](../routes/dfw-lax.md)) | Ultra-short | **30** |
| SFO⇄DFW / SFO⇄ORD | Short | **50** |

Useful padding around a US trip (e.g. while positioned in San Francisco from an
[[strategies/ex-origin-fares]] run). AA domestic business-tier and first-tier pay the
same SP at Ultra-short/Short zones, so the cheapest F-cabin fare wins.

## Other verified ultra-short premium floors

From the sector registries (SP per one-way sector, business tier):

- MH KUL⇄SIN — 184mi, 15 SP ([[airlines/malaysia-airlines-routes]]); regional business
  frequently ~HK$700–1,000
- QF BNE⇄OOL — 58mi(!), 15 SP business / 15 first ([[airlines/qantas-routes]])
- BA LHR⇄EDI/ABZ — Club Europe, 15 SP ([[airlines/british-airways-routes]])

## Caveats

- Fares quoted are indicative 2026 community levels; domestic sales vary by season.
- Only the listed booking classes earn the premium tier — basic/award upsells at the
  gate usually don't change the earning class.
- Remember the annual **one eligible CX sector** requirement — an all-partner year
  earns SP but no status ([[programme/status-tiers]]).
