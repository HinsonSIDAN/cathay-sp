Tactics for maximising Status Points per dollar ("跑分"). Every SP figure in this folder
is **verified against the official Cathay calculator** (same data as [[routes]] and
[[airlines]]); fares quoted are **indicative community-reported levels (2026)** and
volatile — always reprice before committing.

The one metric that matters: **SP per HK$1,000 spent** — see [[strategies/cost-benchmarks]].

## The three levers

1. **Where you start** — the same metal in the same cabin is priced very differently by
   point of sale/origin: [[strategies/ex-origin-fares]]
2. **How many sectors** — SP is a per-sector zone lookup that grows sub-linearly with
   distance, so splitting a journey adds points: [[strategies/sector-splitting]]
3. **Which fare buys the tier** — cheap fares that land in expensive SP tiers (JAL
   Class J, AA domestic First, ultra-short business): [[strategies/short-hop-runs]],
   [[strategies/qr-doha-runs]]

Plus **when**: 2026 is the decisive accumulation year for the 2027 relaunch —
[[strategies/programme-timing]].

## Ground rules (apply to everything here)

- Only **eligible revenue fares** earn; award, most industry/agency and unused tickets
  earn nothing.
- Earning follows the **marketing carrier** — book JL flight numbers for JAL domestic
  runs, QR numbers for Doha runs, etc.
- At least **one eligible CX sector** per membership year is required for status
  (see [[programme/status-tiers]]).
- SP values cited are current as of 2026-07-14 and could change with the 2027 relaunch.
