> **Source:** [Alaska Airlines partner page (cathaypacific.com)](https://www.cathaypacific.com/cx/en_HK/our-partners/alaska-airlines.html) and the official Cathay Status Points & Asia Miles calculator (API-observed, 2026-07-14).

Alaska Airlines is a oneworld member: flights marketed by AS earn **Asia Miles** (rate % × actual miles flown) **and Status Points** (zone lookup by SP tier) when credited to Cathay. Zones and the SP tier matrix are defined in [[earning/partner-model]].

## Earning by booking class

| Cabin | Fare classes | Scope | Asia Miles | SP tier | SP Ultra-short | SP Short | SP Medium-1 | SP Medium-2 | SP Long | SP Ultra-long |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| First | A, C, D, F, I, J | all | 150% | A | 15 | 25 | 50 | 70 | 90 | 100 |
| Economy | B, H, K, L, M, N, S, V, Y | all | 100% | F | 5 | 10 | 20 | 25 | 35 | 40 |
| Economy | G, O, Q | all | 50% | G | 5 | 10 | 15 | 20 | 25 | 30 |

## Eligibility

Asia Miles can be earned only in eligible fare classes on flights marketed and operated by Alaska Airlines or Horizon Air, or on flights marketed and operated by Alaska Airlines and branded as Hawaiian Airlines.
