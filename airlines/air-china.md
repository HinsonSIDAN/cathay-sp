> **Source:** [Air China partner page (cathaypacific.com)](https://www.cathaypacific.com/cx/en_HK/our-partners/air-china.html) and the official Cathay Status Points & Asia Miles calculator (API-observed, 2026-07-14).

Air China is a Cathay airline partner but **not** a oneworld member: flights marketed by CA earn **Asia Miles only** (rate % × actual miles flown) and **zero Status Points** when credited to Cathay (see [[earning/partner-model]]).

## Earning by booking class

| Cabin | Fare classes | Scope | Asia Miles | Status Points |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| First | F | all | 200% | 0 |
| First | A | all | 150% | 0 |
| Business | C, J | all | 150% | 0 |
| Business | D, R, Z | all | 125% | 0 |
| Premium Economy | G | all | 110% | 0 |
| Premium Economy | E | all | 100% | 0 |
| Economy | B, Y | all | 100% | 0 |
| Economy | M, U | all | 75% | 0 |
| Economy | H, Q, S, T, V, W | all | 50% | 0 |
| Economy | K, L, P | all | 25% | 0 |

## Eligibility

Asia Miles can only be earned in eligible fare classes on flights marketed and operated by Air China, as well as on codeshare flights operated by Cathay Pacific.
