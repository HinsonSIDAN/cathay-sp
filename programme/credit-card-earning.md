> and Standard Chartered card terms (via community documentation, retrieved 2026-07-14).

The only way to earn Status Points without flying (HK market only): the
**Standard Chartered Cathay Mastercard**.

## Earning rules

| Rule | Value |
|---|---|
| Rate | **HK$100,000 eligible spend = 20 SP** |
| Increment | 20-SP blocks only — partial progress doesn't pay out, but unmet quarterly amounts roll forward cumulatively |
| Annual cap | **100 SP** (= HK$500,000 spend) |
| Card variants | Standard, Priority Banking, Priority Private Banking — all earn identically |

## Eligible vs excluded spend

- **Counts:** local retail, overseas purchases, online shopping, instalment postings,
  Octopus auto-reload.
- **Excluded:** bill payments / ebanking bills, e-wallet transfers and P2P (FPS/转數快),
  mobile Octopus top-ups, insurance premiums, jewellery/precious-metal wholesale,
  card fees.

## Posting timing (plan around this)

Quarterly with a lag of up to ~3 months:

| Spend quarter | SP posted by |
|---|---|
| Jan–Mar | 30 Jun |
| Apr–Jun | 30 Sep |
| Jul–Sep | 31 Dec |
| Oct–Dec | 31 Mar (next year) |

**Q4 spend posts the following year** — for a year-end status push, card SP from
October–December arrives too late to count for that year's tally. Front-load card spend
to Q1–Q3 if the points matter for the current qualification year (especially 2026, which
determines 2027 status — see [[strategies/programme-timing]]).

## Perspective

100 SP/year ≈ one HKG⇄SFO one-way in Business Light. Useful as a top-up to bridge a
50–100 SP shortfall to a threshold; not a substitute for flying. Card SP do **not**
satisfy the one-eligible-CX-sector requirement ([[programme/status-tiers]]).
